Shokunin — The Craft

Two drawings,moved by your hand.

Scroll slowly. The first drawing assembles a nigiri the way we do every morning — layer by layer. The second reads a whole salmon down to its bones, the way the knife does.

Scroll — the ink follows you
(01) — 組立 The Assembly 000
01 / 05 — シャリ

Two hundred grains, pressed twice.

Koshihikari rice, body temperature, seasoned with red vinegar. Held, not squeezed.

02 / 05 — 山葵

A breath of heat, hidden.

Fresh wasabi goes under the fish, never beside it. You taste it second.

03 / 05 — ネタ

The salmon lands.

Ōra King, cut at eleven degrees so the fat striping faces the light.

04 / 05 — 海苔

Wrapped once, held.

A single band of toasted nori — structure first, aroma second.

05 / 05 — 仕上げ

Brushed, sealed, served.

Nikiri glaze, a scatter of sesame, and the seal of the house. Eighteen grams. One bite.

Every piece begins the same way — with a whole fish, and a reason for every cut.

(02) — 解剖 The Anatomy 000
01 / 04 — 姿

Whole. The silver side.

One Ōra King salmon, iced at four in the morning. This is where every menu item starts.

02 / 04 — 庖丁

The knife knows the map.

Flesh lifts from bone in two long passes. Keep scrolling — the drawing reads the fish for you.

03 / 04 — 脊椎

Sixty vertebrae, one line.

Ribs forward, tail spines aft. Pin bones pulled one by one with tweezers.

04 / 04 — 骨

What remains is structure.

The frame becomes tomorrow's stock. Nothing hidden, nothing wasted.

That is the whole trick — there is no trick. Just the same careful sequence, repeated for every guest you invite.
